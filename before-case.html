Today’s Aim

Recognise dangerous chest pain and act before certainty.

What This Pattern Is Testing

Can I recognise possible heart-related chest pain, stay calm, and arrange urgent care safely?

This is not a diagnosis-list station.

This is a safety-sorting station.

How This Pattern May Appear

This pattern may appear as:

  • Patient consultation
  • Investigation interpretation
  • Physical examination
  • Acute emergency management
  • Refusal or counselling conversation

You do not choose the station type.

The case will tell you what to do.

Focus Here

  • Confirm the setting.
  • Check if the patient is unstable.
  • Look for immediate threat signs.
  • Do not reassure early.

Main Danger

Acute coronary syndrome.

Patient wording:

“Possible heart-related chest pain.”

Four Causes Not to Miss

  • Acute coronary syndrome
  • Aortic dissection
  • Pulmonary embolism
  • Tension pneumothorax

Fast Trigger

Chest pressure with sweating, nausea, breathlessness, radiation, collapse, or cardiac risk factors.

Treat this as a possible heart problem until proven otherwise.

First Action in General Practice

Call ambulance now.

Do an electrocardiogram immediately if available and not delaying transfer.

Do Not Do These

  • Do not call it reflux too early.
  • Do not delay transfer for troponin.
  • Do not let the patient drive.
  • Do not give oxygen routinely.
  • Do not give glyceryl trinitrate without checking blood pressure and medication safety.

One Sentence to Remember

Chest pressure with sweating, nausea, breathlessness, radiation, collapse, or cardiac risk factors is a possible heart problem until proven otherwise.
