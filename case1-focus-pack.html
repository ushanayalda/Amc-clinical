Hint: Missed diagnosis

What was missed
You did not clearly recognise that this could be a heart problem.
Why it matters
Mr Lewis has ongoing chest pressure with arm and jaw spread, sweating, nausea, breathlessness, and heart risk factors.
Say this
“I am treating this as a possible heart problem now.”
Practise now
Say the 10-second trigger rule once without medical jargon.

Section 3 - Phase 6 - Diagnosis Explanation.

Hint: Used too many medical words

What was missed
You used hospital language with the patient.
Why it matters
Mr Lewis needs clear urgent language, not terms like acute coronary syndrome, biomarkers, or risk stratification.
Say this
“The main concern is that this may be coming from your heart.”
Practise now
Replace "acute coronary syndrome" with "possible heart problem" three times.
Go back to
Section 3 - Phase 6 - Diagnosis Explanation.

Hint: Missed another serious cause

What was missed
You did not mention the other serious causes hospital must check.
Why it matters
Chest pain can come from the heart, the main blood vessel, the lung, or a collapsed lung.
Say this
“There are a few other serious causes of chest pain that hospital also needs to check.”
Practise now
Say the four causes not to miss in one breath.
Go back to
Section 3 - Phase 7 - Differential Explanation.

Hint: Missed danger questions

What was missed
You did not ask the short danger questions.
Why it matters
You need to quickly check spread of pain, sweating, nausea, breathlessness, tearing back pain, clot risk, coughing blood, and sudden sharp breathlessness.
Say this
“Thank you. I will keep the questions focused.”
Practise now
Ask the Must Ask list aloud in 45 seconds.
Go back to
Section 3 - Phase 3 - Focused History.

Hint: Missed why you acted now

What was missed
You did not explain why you were acting before confirmation.
Why it matters
Ongoing chest pressure with spread, sweating, nausea, breathlessness, and risk factors is enough to act now.
Say this
“I am not waiting for certainty before acting.”
Practise now
Give the Decision Point in 15 seconds using Mr Lewis’ features.
Go back to
Section 3 - Phase 8A - Decision Point.

Hint: Missed first action

What was missed
You did not arrange the ambulance early.
Why it matters
General Practice cannot safely rule this out, and transfer is the first safe action.
Say this
“I am arranging an ambulance now.”
Practise now
Start the station with ambulance action before the full history.
Go back to
Section 3 - Phase 1 - Introduction and Consent.

Hint: Missed ambulance clarity

What was missed
You allowed private transport or made the ambulance sound optional.
Why it matters
The ambulance can monitor him and act quickly if his condition changes.
Say this
“You should not drive yourself.”
Practise now
Answer "Can my wife drive me?" in two short sentences.
Go back to
Section 3 - Phase 8B - Management Plan.

Hint: Waited for tests

What was missed
You waited for blood tests or over-investigated before transfer.
Why it matters
Troponin and other blood tests belong in hospital and must not delay the ambulance.
Say this
“I will not delay the ambulance for blood tests that need to happen in hospital.”
Practise now
Say the ECG rule: heart tracing only if it does not delay transfer.
Go back to
Section 3 - Phase 8B - Management Plan.

Hint: Missed medicine safety

What was missed
You gave or promised treatment without safety checks.
Why it matters
Aspirin, oxygen, and chest pain spray or tablet all need safe use.
Say this
“Before any chest pain spray or tablet under the tongue, I will check your blood pressure and make sure none of your recent medicines make it unsafe.”
Practise now
Say aspirin safety, oxygen safety, and spray safety in 30 seconds.
Go back to
Section 3 - Phase 8B - Management Plan.

Hint: Missed warning signs

What was missed
You did not tell Mr Lewis what to report while waiting.
Why it matters
He can get worse before the ambulance arrives.
Say this
“Tell me immediately if the pain gets worse, you feel faint, become more breathless, feel very clammy, or feel like you might collapse.”
Practise now
Say the full warning line once, then shorten it to 15 seconds.
Go back to
Section 3 - Phase 10 - Safety Net.

Hint: Missed understanding check

What was missed
You did not check that the patient understood the ambulance decision.
Why it matters
Understanding reduces resistance and confirms the urgent plan.
Say this
“Can you tell me what you understand about why I am arranging an ambulance?”
Practise now
Ask the understanding check and respond to Mr Lewis’ answer.
Go back to
Section 3 - Phase 11 - Check Understanding.

Hint: Ran out of time

What was missed
You spent too long on background history or textbook explanation.
Why it matters
This station rewards early action, focused questions, short explanation, and transfer.
Say this
“Thank you. I have enough information to act safely now.”
Practise now
Run Section 7 with a hard 8-minute stop.

What was missed

Why it matters
