SECTION 1 – STATION STEM

You are a GP in a suburban clinic.

A 34-year-old woman presents with sudden right-sided chest pain and shortness of breath for 3 hours.

Past medical history: Recent long-haul flight 4 days ago.
Medications: Combined oral contraceptive pill.

Tasks:

  1. Take a focused history.
  2. Explain your most likely diagnosis and differential diagnoses.
  3. Outline your management plan.

You have 8 minutes.

SECTION 3 – SPOKEN SCRIPT

PHASE 1 – Introduction and Consent

Hello, I am Dr Ushana.
How would you like me to address you?
Patient: “Call me Sara.”
Thank you Sara. I understand you have sudden chest pain and shortness of breath. I will ask some focused questions and then explain what I think and what we need to do next. Is that okay?
Patient: “Yes.”

PHASE 2 – Open Question

Can you tell me what happened today?
Patient: “I suddenly felt sharp pain in my chest and it hurts to breathe.”

PHASE 3 – Focused History

When did it begin?
Patient: “About three hours ago.”
Was it sudden?
Patient: “Yes.”
Where exactly is the pain?
Patient: “Right side.”
Is it worse when you breathe in?
Patient: “Yes.”
Any coughing up blood?
Patient: “No.”
Any fainting?
Patient: “No.”
Any swelling or pain in your legs?
Patient: “My left calf feels sore.”
Have you travelled recently?
Patient: “Yes, long flight four days ago.”
Are you on the oral contraceptive pill?
Patient: “Yes.”
Any severe tearing pain to your back?
Patient: “No.”
Any heavy sweating or radiation to your arm or jaw?
Patient: “No.”
Are you allergic to any medications?
Patient: “No.”

PHASE 4 – Red Flag Sweep

Just to confirm, no collapse, no severe sudden tearing back pain, and no confusion. Correct?
Patient: “Correct.”

PHASE 5 – Structured Summary

Let me summarise to ensure I understood correctly.
You developed sudden sharp right-sided chest pain three hours ago, worse with breathing, associated with shortness of breath. You recently travelled long-haul, are on the oral contraceptive pill, and have some calf discomfort. Is that accurate?
Patient: “Yes.”

PHASE 6 – Diagnosis Explanation

Based on what you have told me, the most likely diagnosis is a pulmonary embolism, which is a blood clot in the lungs.
SCORING PHRASE Sudden pleuritic chest pain with shortness of breath and risk factors must be treated as pulmonary embolism until proven otherwise.

PHASE 7 – Differential Explanation

Other possibilities include:
  • Pneumothorax
  • Acute coronary syndrome
However, your risk factors strongly support pulmonary embolism.

PHASE 8 – Management Plan

We are in a GP setting.
FOCUS HERE
This cannot be safely investigated here.
You require urgent hospital assessment immediately.
I will arrange ambulance transfer now.
We will not delay transfer for blood tests in clinic.
Oxygen will be provided only if your oxygen level is low or you develop respiratory distress.
SCORING PHRASE Suspected pulmonary embolism in GP requires urgent hospital referral without delay.
Before any blood thinners are started in hospital, contraindications such as bleeding risk will be assessed.

PHASE 9 – Urgency Classification

This is an emergency and requires immediate hospital transfer.

PHASE 10 – Safety Net

If your breathing worsens or you feel faint while waiting, emergency services are already being arranged and we will act immediately.

PHASE 11 – Check Understanding

Do you have any questions before we arrange the ambulance?
Patient: “No.”
