Case 2 - Atypical ACS in diabetic - Strict AMC format

Section 1 - Station Stem

You are a GP in a suburban clinic.

A 62-year-old woman presents with upper abdominal discomfort and breathlessness for 2 hours.

Past medical history

  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Hypertension

Medications

  • Metformin
  • Ramipril

Tasks

  • Take a focused history.
  • Explain your most likely diagnosis and differential diagnoses.
  • Outline your management plan.

You have 8 minutes.

Section 2 - 2 Minute Reading Map

Setting

GP

Context

  • Diabetic patient
  • Atypical symptoms
  • Epigastric discomfort

Immediate threats to exclude

  • Acute coronary syndrome
  • Aortic dissection
  • Pulmonary embolism

First action rule

ECG immediately

FOCUS HERE
Atypical does not mean safe.

Urgency

Emergency transfer

Section 3 - Spoken Script

SPOKEN

Phase 1 - Introduction and Consent

Hello, I am Dr Ushana.

How would you like me to address you?

Patient: “Call me Maria.”

Thank you Maria. I understand you have upper abdominal discomfort and breathlessness. I will ask some focused questions and then explain what I think and what we need to do next. Is that okay?

Patient: “Yes.”

Phase 2 - Open Question

Can you tell me what happened today?

Patient: “I started feeling uncomfortable in my upper abdomen and short of breath.”

Phase 3 - Focused History

When did this begin?

Patient: “About two hours ago.”

Was it sudden or gradual?

Patient: “Sudden.”

How would you describe the discomfort?

Patient: “Tight.”

Does it spread anywhere?

Patient: “Not really.”

Any sweating or nausea?

Patient: “Yes, nausea.”

Any chest pressure?

Patient: “Maybe a little.”

Did it start during exertion?

Patient: “Yes, while walking.”

Does it improve with rest?

Patient: “Not fully.”

Any fainting?

Patient: “No.”

Any tearing pain to your back?

Patient: “No.”

Any recent travel or leg swelling?

Patient: “No.”

Are you allergic to aspirin?

Patient: “No.”

Have you taken any erectile dysfunction medication in the last 48 hours?

Patient: “No.”

Phase 4 - Red Flag Sweep

Just to confirm, no collapse, no severe tearing pain to the back, and no confusion. Correct?

Patient: “Correct.”

Phase 5 - Structured Summary

Let me summarise to ensure I understood correctly.

You developed sudden upper abdominal discomfort with some tightness and nausea two hours ago while walking. You are diabetic and hypertensive. The discomfort has not fully resolved with rest. Is that accurate?

Patient: “Yes.”

Phase 6 - Diagnosis Explanation

Even though your symptoms are not classic chest pain, in a person with diabetes these symptoms can represent acute coronary syndrome.

SCORING PHRASE
In diabetic patients, acute coronary syndrome may present atypically without classic chest pain.

Phase 7 - Differential Explanation

Other possibilities include:

  • Gastro-oesophageal reflux
  • Pulmonary embolism

However, because of your diabetes and exertional onset, I must treat this as a possible cardiac emergency.

Phase 8 - Management Plan

We are in a GP setting.

FOCUS HERE
Atypical does not mean safe.

The first step is to perform an ECG immediately.

SCORING PHRASE
ECG must be done immediately in suspected acute coronary syndrome, even with atypical presentation.

I will give aspirin now since you have no allergy.

You require urgent ambulance transfer to hospital for further evaluation including troponin testing.

We will not delay transfer for investigations that can only be performed in hospital.

I will provide oxygen only if your oxygen level is low or you become distressed.

Phase 9 - Urgency Classification

This is an emergency requiring immediate hospital transfer.

Phase 10 - Safety Net

If your symptoms worsen, you feel faint, or breathing becomes more difficult, emergency services are already being called and we will act immediately.

Phase 11 - Check Understanding

Do you have any questions before we arrange the ambulance?

Patient: “No.”

Section 4 - Internal Thinking - Not Spoken

INTERNAL THINKING - NOT SPOKEN
  • Diabetic patient
  • Exertional onset
  • Nausea
  • Atypical ACS likely
  • Do not anchor on reflux
  • Immediate ECG
  • Immediate transfer
  • No reflex oxygen

Section 5 - Scoring Phrases

SCORING PHRASE LIST
