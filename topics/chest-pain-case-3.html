Case 3 - Pulmonary embolism

Section 1 - Station Stem

You are a GP in a suburban clinic.

A 34-year-old woman presents with sudden right-sided chest pain and shortness of breath for 3 hours.

Past history: Recent long-haul flight 4 days ago.

Medications: Combined oral contraceptive pill.

Tasks:

  • Take a focused history.
  • Explain your most likely diagnosis and differential diagnoses.
  • Outline your management plan.

Section 2 - 2 Minute Reading Map

  • Sudden pleuritic pain
  • Shortness of breath
  • OCP
  • Recent flight

Likely direction: Pulmonary embolism

Urgency: Emergency transfer

Section 3 - Spoken Script

Introduction: Hello, I am Dr Ushana. How would you like me to address you?

Patient: Call me Sara.

Sudden pleuritic pain and breathlessness discussed.

Risk factors explored including OCP and recent travel.

Calf pain noted.

Summary: Sudden pleuritic chest pain, breathlessness, OCP, recent flight.

Sudden pleuritic chest pain with risk factors must be treated as pulmonary embolism until proven otherwise.

Management: Immediate ambulance transfer from GP setting. No delay for in-clinic tests.

Section 4 - Internal Thinking - Not Spoken

  • Sudden pleuritic pain
  • OCP
  • Recent travel
  • Calf pain
  • High PE probability
  • Immediate referral

Section 5 - Scoring Phrases

  • Pleuritic chest pain with risk factors suggests pulmonary embolism.
  • Do not delay hospital referral in suspected PE.
  • Oxygen only if hypoxic or distressed.
